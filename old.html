Old Movies

Movies from 1900s-1950s

Seven Samurai

Review

Somehow down-to-earth yet stunningly epic, this film captivated me. It's as uplifting as it is tragic - a tale of idealism in a cynical world.

The Cabinent of Dr. Caligari

Review

Striking visuals that transcend the limitations of its time, with an eerie psychological horror underlying it. It's a classic for a reason.

Citizen Kane

Review

Beautifully shot, incredibly acted, and with a message about corruption and greed that still resonates more than 80 years later. While the jury is still out if it's "the greatest film of all time", I can see why many would give it that title.

Rear Window

Review

While undeniably dated in some respects - the main character gets to be a jerk with little to no consequences- it is still a highly engaging suspsense thriller.

Forbidden Planet

Review

This film is the inspiration of so much of the modern Sci-Fi genre, including Star Trek and Star Wars. The effects are still decent considering the time period, but the characters suffer the worst from the film's age. None of them have much depth, and they simply serve as vehicles for the film's somewhat intereting mystery.
